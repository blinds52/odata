Notes on Structure of Display and Symbols Used

We divide all action items into active and closed actions. The active actions are displayed first, while the closed ones are displayed second.

Inside each of the two main sections, there are upto three tables. These tables map each action items processing stages as documented in meeting minutes to the corresponding section of the publicly accessible meeting minute document on the OASIS servers. Each action item is mapped into one of the three tables of its main section as derived from the priorities offered by Kavi:

  1. Class 1 Actions: Priority = High
  2. Class 2 Actions: Priority = Medium
  3. Class 3 Actions: Priority = Low

The tooltips on the inner cells assist in indicating the date of the meeting. The link targets of the inner cells point to the relevant subsection in the meeting document. the summary row at the bottom of each table yields the count of action items processed during that meeting and is linked with the meeting document. Each row can be addressed individually by appending a fragment like eg. "#ai0123" (for an action item with ID="AI#0123") to the URL of this document. The background color of the leftmost columns cell w.r.t. Action-IDs somehow encodes the status as received from Kavi and is one of the two states: Open or Closed. This document is updated after each meeting and after approval of the relevant minute document by the TC and is provided in the hope, it is useful.
